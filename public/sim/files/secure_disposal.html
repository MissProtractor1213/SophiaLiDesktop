Secure Disposal & Sensitive Paper Handling

Northbridge Solutions • Information Governance
Doc ID: NB-IG-204
Version: 1.6
Last reviewed: Aug 15, 2025

TL;DR: Put sensitive papers in a locked blue shred bin or use an approved cross‑cut shredder (P‑4+). Never toss work documents into regular trash or recycling. When unsure, shred.

Why secure disposal matters

Gather the scraps, only then will the book open. If you find the four digits, the door shall open.

Dumpsters are public once placed for pickup. Improper disposal exposes customer data, trade secrets, employee records, and credentials. That means legal risk, fines, and brand damage that can start with a single sheet of paper.

What counts as sensitive?

  • Anything with names, emails, phone numbers, or addresses
  • Financial figures, pricing, budgets, or invoices
  • HR files: performance reviews, offers, medical notes
  • Customer lists, contracts, NDAs, RFPs
  • System info: network diagrams, IPs, passwords (never print!)

What belongs in the blue shred bin

  • Stapled or paper‑clipped packets (no need to remove)
  • Envelopes (plastic windows OK)
  • Notebooks, sticky notes, index cards
  • Printed labels & shipping docs

Approved disposal methods

1) Locked shred bins

Located on every floor near copy rooms. Vendor empties weekly. If full, request pickup via Helpdesk → Facilities.

2) Cross‑cut shredders (P‑4 or higher)

Use only approved models. Tip Empty shredder bags into the locked bin after use—never into regular trash.

3) On‑site purge events

Quarterly clean‑outs for boxes of records. Keep boxes sealed; label with team, owner, and date.

Good to know: You don’t have to remove staples, clips, or small binder clips. Shredders and vendor equipment handle them.
Never do this: Place printed passwords, access badges, or device labels in normal trash or mixed recycling.

Disposal workflow (office)

  1. Sort your pile: keep, digitize (scan), or dispose.
  2. Remove non‑paper items larger than a binder clip (e.g., discs).
  3. Place documents in a locked blue shred bin or use an approved shredder.
  4. For boxes (≥ 20 lbs), schedule a purge pickup.
  5. Record retention question? Ask Legal/Records before shredding.

Working from home

Use company‑issued shred bags or an at‑home cross‑cut shredder (P‑4+). Store unshredded documents in a closed container; bring filled shred bags to the office within 7 days.

WFH reminder: Never place company documents in residential recycling or curbside trash—even if shredded. Bring to office bins.

Non‑paper media

Drives & SSDs

Create an IT ticket for certified destruction. Wipe is not enough for end‑of‑life assets.

USBs, SD cards, phones

Turn in at IT service desk; do not place in shred bins.

Badges & access cards

Return to Security. Do not cut, bend, or toss in trash.

Myth vs. Fact

Myth

“I tore it in half—no one will piece it together.”

Fact

Reconstruction is easy with modern tools. Only cross‑cut shredding and certified destruction mitigate the risk.

Quick FAQ

Do I need to remove staples?

No. Staples and paper clips are OK.

Is regular “strip‑cut” shredding acceptable?

No. Use cross‑cut or micro‑cut (security level P‑4 or higher).

Can I recycle shredded paper?

Shredded paper goes into locked bins only; the vendor handles recycling after secure processing.
