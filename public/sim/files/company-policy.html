1. Purpose & Scope

This handbook defines behavioral, security, and operational standards for all workforce members. It applies to Company-managed systems, data, facilities, and any third-party service used on behalf of the Company.

Plain language: Do the right thing, protect customer data, and ask when unsure.

2. Code of Conduct

  • Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect. Harassment and discrimination are prohibited.
  • Disclose any conflicts of interest to your manager and Compliance.
  • Use Company assets primarily for work purposes.
  • Report suspected misconduct through one of: your manager, HR, or ethics@northbridge.example.

3. Data Handling & Classification

All information must be classified and protected accordingly:

ClassDescriptionExamplesMinimum Controls
Public Intended for public release. Marketing site content, press releases. Review before release.
Internal Non-public operational info. Org charts, non-sensitive metrics. Company email only; share as needed.
Confidential Business-sensitive data. Financials, roadmap, contracts. Encrypt at rest & in transit, limited access.
Restricted Highest sensitivity (legal/regulatory). Customer PII, credentials, keys. MFA required, need-to-know, DLP monitoring.
Note: Customer data is at least Confidential; authentication secrets are Restricted.

4. Accounts, Passwords & MFA

  • Use unique passwords; minimum 14 characters; prefer a password manager.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever available.
  • Never share credentials. Service accounts must use keys/tokens kept in a secret manager.
  • Lock your device when unattended (Win+L or Ctrl+Cmd+Q).
Prohibited: Storing passwords in plain text, emailing credentials, or reusing passwords across services.

5. Devices & Endpoint Security

  • Only Company-managed devices may access Restricted data.
  • Keep OS and software updated; do not disable EDR/antivirus.
  • Use full-disk encryption on laptops and mobile devices.
  • Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT within 1 hour.

6. Email, Internet & Messaging

  • Use Company email for business. Do not auto-forward to personal accounts.
  • Beware of phishing. Verify unexpected requests for money, data, or credentials.
  • Only approved file sharing tools may be used for Company data.
  • Be professional in chat tools—assume messages may be discoverable.
Phishing check: Inspect sender, links, urgency, and attachments. When in doubt, report to phish@northbridge.example.

7. Remote & Hybrid Work

  • Connect via Company VPN when accessing internal systems.
  • Do not use public Wi-Fi without a VPN. Avoid working with sensitive data in public spaces.
  • Store work files in approved cloud drives; local copies should be temporary.

8. Incident Reporting

Report security incidents immediately. Examples include: lost devices, suspected malware, unauthorized access, data exposure, or policy violations.

9. Physical Security & Visitors

  • Wear your badge visibly; challenge unbadged visitors or notify Security.
  • Escort visitors at all times; ensure they sign in and out.
  • Keep doors closed—do not hold them open for unknown persons (“tailgating”).

10. Clean Desk & Printing

  • Lock screens and drawers when away from your desk.
  • Collect sensitive print jobs immediately; use secure print release if available.
  • Shred documents marked Confidential or higher using cross-cut shredders.

11. Security Awareness & Training

Mandatory training is assigned at hire and annually thereafter. Phishing simulations may be conducted without notice.

12. Exceptions & Approvals

To request a policy exception, submit a ticket with business justification, scope, compensating controls, and time limit. Approvals require Security and the data owner.

Time-boxed: Exceptions should not exceed 90 days without re-approval.
