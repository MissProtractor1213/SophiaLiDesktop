Local Man Declares Inbox “Handled” After Bold, Decisive Move To Mark All As Read

Area productivity icon achieves Inbox Zero* in record time; asterisk later clarified to mean “emotionally.”

By Poppy Quill • The Parsnip
Tuesday — 9:17 AM
Productivity
Urban Triumphs

In a pioneering act of digital courage Monday morning, local resident **Trent Bolivar** announced he had “handled” his inbox—seconds after discovering the button that marks every message as read, sources who definitely aren’t his manager confirmed.

“I felt the surge of productivity ripple through the office Slack,” said Trent, tapping a sticky note labeled “Do One Thing.” “And I did. It just happened to be a thing that erased all evidence of the other things.”

Experts Applaud, Then Cautiously Ask About The 43 Follow-Up Threads

Productivity researchers at the Dubious Institute for Human Momentum categorized Trent’s feat as a “Category 5 Procrastination Cyclone,” noting that while unread counts plummeted 100%, the number of unresolved tasks held steady at “all of them.”

“It’s a classic Schrödinger’s Email scenario,” said Dr. Lola Quantum, opening a laptop that made the sound of a far-off sigh. “If you never look, every message is simultaneously handled and not your problem.”

Colleagues Report Strange Calm, Followed By 14,000 “Per My Last Email”s

Trent’s team initially celebrated the absence of red notification bubbles, describing it as “what we imagine peace feels like.” That calm reportedly ended when fifteen threads titled Quick Ping resurfaced, each containing a 37-point project plan.

“I admire the efficiency,” said teammate Sumi, forwarding a time-sensitive spreadsheet for the fourth time. “But we were hoping he’d maybe open the one about the mold in the break room.”

Hero Accepts Key To The City’s Spam Folder

At a brief ceremony held between the espresso machine and a potted succulent named Janet, Trent received a ceremonial key to the city’s spam folder and a commemorative plaque reading, “Inbox Zero (Vibes Edition).”

Asked what’s next, the trailblazer outlined an ambitious roadmap: “First, I will mute all channels. Then, I will turn off desktop notifications. Finally, I will become unreachable between the hours of now and later.”

At press time, sources reported Trent bravely confronting his calendar with the same technique by setting every meeting to “Tentative,” thereby achieving Schedule Zero, a controversial state in which all obligations are both accepted and entirely theoretical.
